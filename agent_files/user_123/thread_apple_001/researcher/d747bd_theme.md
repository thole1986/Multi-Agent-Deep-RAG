## What were Apple's gross margins and operating expenses trends in 2023 and 2024?

### Focused Query 1: Apple gross margin and operating expenses fiscal year 2023 10-K
**Key Findings:**
- **Total Gross Margin (2023):** $169,148 million, or **44.1%** of total net sales (up from 43.3% in 2022).
- **Products Gross Margin (2023):** 36.5% (slight increase from 36.3% in 2022).
- **Services Gross Margin (2023):** 70.8% (decrease from 71.7% in 2022).
- **Operating Expenses (2023):** Totaled $54,847 million, a 7% increase year-over-year.
  - **Research and Development (R&D):** $29,915 million (up 14%), driven by headcount-related expenses.
  - **Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A):** $24,932 million (down 1%), remaining relatively flat.

### Focused Query 2: Apple gross margin and operating expenses fiscal year 2024 10-K
**Key Findings:**
- **Total Gross Margin (2024):** $180,683 million, or **46.2%** of total net sales.
- **Products Gross Margin (2024):** 37.2% (up from 36.5% in 2023).
- **Services Gross Margin (2024):** 73.9% (up from 70.8% in 2023).
- **Operating Expenses (2024):** Totaled $57,467 million, a 5% increase year-over-year.
  - **Research and Development (R&D):** $31,370 million (up 5%), again driven by headcount-related expenses.
  - **Selling, General and Administrative (SG&A):** $26,097 million (up 5%), an increase of $1.2 billion compared to 2023.

### Summary
Apple demonstrated strong margin expansion between 2023 and 2024. The **total gross margin percentage rose from 44.1% to 46.2%**, primarily fueled by cost savings and a favorable mix in Services. Services gross margin saw a significant rebound in 2024, jumping from 70.8% to 73.9%. 

Operating expenses continued to climb, though at a decelerating pace (7% growth in 2023 vs. 5% in 2024). Research and Development remains the primary driver of expense growth, reflecting Apple's continued investment in human capital and innovation, while SG&A expenses returned to growth in 2024 after a flat 2023. Overall, Apple managed to increase its profitability margins despite macroeconomic headwinds like currency fluctuations.
